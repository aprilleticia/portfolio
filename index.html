Why do people work in public media?

I'm managing a project that marks the 50th anniversary of the Public Broadcasting Act by illuminating the experiences that inspired people to choose to work in public media. Every week, we're posting individual stories and promoting them by using the hashtag #IAmPublicMedia. In addition to collecting contributions and sharing them via our social media platforms, I'm using the project to provide live coverage of industry conferences.

Small, midsize stations make progress with major-donor fundraising

What’s a major gift in public media? Depends on who you ask

At most stations, individuals who contribute at least $1,000 cumulatively over a year are considered major donors. But outside of public media, major gifts involve much larger donations. This two-part series identified a weak point in public media fundraising, and covered creative efforts among small and midsize stations to grow their pools of major donors.

A soon-to-be college grad wonders where to start in public radio

“Do I really have to start in the middle of nowhere to eventually get into the big-league public radio game?” a reader asked. Recruiters, our audience and other public radio professionals answered her question. The reader submitted her questions through audience-engagement platform Hearken, which recognized the project with a 2016 Champioins of Curiosity award for Most Public-Powered Reporting Process. Hearken cited Current for bringing "together multiple voices and perspectives to add texture and depth" to conversation around a controversial question.
